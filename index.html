About Me

I am a PhD Candidate in Computer Science Engineering at the University of New South Wales (UNSW) and an associated researcher with CSIRO's Data61. My current research is focused on Privacy-Enhancing Technologies (PETs), specifically investigating the privacy attacks and defenses on AI systems to ensure data security and privacy in distributed AI systems.

My academic foundation includes an M.Tech from IIT Guwahati, where my thesis explored privacy preserving search in federated learning. This foundational work in secure distributed systems prepared me for the complex privacy challenges in modern AI.

Before beginning my doctoral journey, I spent 5 years as a Software Engineer in industry, specializing in scalable data platforms. My roles at Walmart Global Tech, Alation, and Zaloni gave me extensive practical experience in designing and implementing high-throughput data pipelines. This engineering background ensures my research remains grounded in practical, deployable solutions.

I am strongly committed to open science and ensuring the reproducibility of research. I welcome opportunities to collaborate on innovative projects and share my expertise with both the academic community and industry partners.
